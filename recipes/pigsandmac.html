Macaroni and Cheese and Pigs in a Blanket

Description

When you need to feel like a kid again, this is the best way to make it happen. Some simple boxed macaroni and cheese pairs with delicious crescent-roll-wrapped hot dogs, or cheese dogs if that's your preference, to make this a simple yet delightful and very homey meal.

Ready in just 20 minutes, you can find all these ingredients for about 10$ (if you don't already have them in your fridge). This is a great one to teach your kids to make on their own whether it's a day you are too tired to cook or they are on their own for dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1 box Kraft Macaroni and Cheese
    • 4 tablespoons of butter
    • 1/4 cup of milk
  • 1 tube (8 rolls) Pillsbury Butter Flake Crescent Rolls
  • 8 hot dogs (it was always Oscar Mayer Cheese Dogs for me, growing up)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Lay parchment paper or use cooking spray on a sheet pan for the pigs and blankets.
  3. Remove crescent rolls and hot dogs from packages, and lay one hot dog parallel to the shortest edge of each unrolled crescent roll.
  4. Wrap each crescent roll around its hot dog, squeezing and rolling the crescent roll afterward to make an even casing for almost the whole length of its hot dog.
  5. Evenly space the eight pigs in blankets around the prepared sheet pan. I prefer two rows of 4, each at roughly 45 degree angle.
  6. When the oven is preheated, insert the pan on the middle rack and bake them for 10-12 minutes. The tops and bottoms should begin to look golden-brown.
  7. While the pigs in blankets bake. Prepare the macaroni and cheese per the instructions on the Kraft box. NOTE: Add the butter to the pot before returning the strained macaroni - this will help the butter melt. Then, add the cheese powder, followed by the milk to help the powder incorporate into a sauce instead of clumping together.
  8. Once the pigs are sufficiently baked and the macaroni and cheese is complete, divide them onto plates in desired serving sizes!
